# CoachFace Weekly Rewards Plan

## Recommended reward structure

### 1. Every player earns something
- Weekly participation XP for submitting a valid roster before lock.
- Profile badges for achievements such as **Perfect Read**, **Comeback Pick**, **Multi-Sport Master**, and **Top 10%**.
- Streaks for consecutive active weeks and consecutive winning weeks.
- A permanent trophy case showing weekly finishes, league titles, and best sport.

### 2. Strong finishes earn usable value
- Top 25%: CoachFace Points and a weekly profile badge.
- Top 10%: more CoachFace Points plus one discounted contest entry.
- Top 3: premium badge, featured leaderboard placement, merchandise or sponsor prize, and a free future contest entry.
- First place: **CoachFace Weekly Champion** title, trophy animation, shareable winner card, merchandise, and the largest contest reward.

CoachFace Points should initially be promotional loyalty points, not withdrawable cash. Users can redeem them for contest tickets, merchandise discounts, profile customization, and special tournaments.

### 3. Paid contest prize pools
For each paid contest, publish the entry fee, guaranteed prize pool, payout table, roster rules, scoring rules, lock time, and tiebreaker before entry.

Suggested payout model for standard global contests:
- 1st: 30% of the prize pool
- 2nd: 18%
- 3rd: 12%
- 4th through 10th: 25% combined
- Remaining paid positions: 15% combined

Also support flatter payout contests where the top 20% win smaller amounts. This gives casual users a better chance of receiving a reward.

## Competition formats

### Global leaderboard
- Weekly free global contest for reach and engagement.
- Separate paid global contests at several entry levels.
- Overall leaderboard plus sport-specific rankings.

### Private leagues
- Friends, offices, and communities create free or paid season-long leagues.
- Weekly winner badges, season standings, commissioner settings, and optional league-funded prizes.

### Head-to-head
- One opponent, one weekly roster, winner takes the published prize after any platform fee.
- Support private challenges and skill-matched public opponents.
- Ties refund entries or split the prize according to rules shown before entry.

## Scoring principle

A coach’s fantasy score must reflect both coaching decisions and the performance of the athletes under that coach.

Use a transparent blended score:
- 45% team or athlete performance: results, efficiency, execution, improvement against expectation.
- 35% coaching decisions: tactics, substitutions, challenges, lineup choices, clock management, and adjustments.
- 20% context: opponent strength, injuries, away conditions, tournament round, and pre-game expectations.

For individual sports such as golf and tennis, player performance is attributed to the selected coach using improvement versus baseline, execution in decisive moments, and tournament result. Every awarded point should link to its underlying event and published rule.

## Fairness and safeguards

Before real-money launch:
- Require age and identity verification, location eligibility, contest limits, self-exclusion, and responsible-play controls.
- Exclude coaches, athletes, team employees, and anyone with confidential competitive information.
- Publish cancellation, postponement, stat-correction, tie, refund, and abandoned-game rules.
- Keep prize settlement pending until all relevant scores are final.
- Confirm fantasy-contest legality and payment eligibility for every supported jurisdiction before accepting paid entries.

## Product implementation

1. Add a **Rewards** area showing weekly prizes, CoachFace Points, badges, streaks, contest tickets, and trophy history.
2. Extend contests with free or paid entry type, entry price, guaranteed prize pool, payout table, eligibility rules, and settlement status.
3. Add reward ledger and prize-award records so every point, ticket, badge, merchandise reward, and cash payout is auditable.
4. Add all three competition types: global, private league, and head-to-head.
5. Update the scoring model so athlete or team performance contributes explicitly to each coach’s score, with an explainable breakdown.
6. Launch free rewards and sponsor merchandise first, then activate paid contests only after compliance, payment, and payout verification are complete.
7. Update current copy that says “free-to-play” and “no cash entry contests” once paid contests are approved and available.
